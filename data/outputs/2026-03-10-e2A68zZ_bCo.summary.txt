This video discusses the issue of RBC using 'blanket appraisals' to bypass the legal 80% loan-to-value limit on mortgages, effectively lending more than the property's current market value. Despite these practices being known for years, Canada's banking regulator only recently issued a warning, leading RBC to make superficial website changes without stopping the practice. The video highlights concerns about systemic risk, government tolerance of risky lending to protect big banks and the real estate market, and the potential for ongoing moral hazard. It emphasizes that the government is effectively picking winners and losers in the real estate industry, undermining market discipline.
