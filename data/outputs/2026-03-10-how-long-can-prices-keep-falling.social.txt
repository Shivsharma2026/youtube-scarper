Option 1: Housing weakness is no longer just a Toronto story. The latest market discussion points to falling prices, softer sales, and rising strain across multiple provinces, with oversupply in rentals adding new pressure. The near-term outlook still looks defensive until demand, financing, and inventory move back into balance.

Option 2: The most important signal in this market may be the combination of weak sales today and weaker construction tomorrow. If falling prices, rising vacancies, and financing pressure continue at the same time, the adjustment could last longer than many expected. That creates a difficult backdrop for buyers, builders, lenders, and renters alike.

Option 3: Canadian housing is facing pressure from several directions at once: price declines, rental oversupply in key pockets, slower household formation, and uncertainty around rates and growth. None of those issues is decisive on its own. Together, they explain why the market still looks heavy even after a meaningful correction.
