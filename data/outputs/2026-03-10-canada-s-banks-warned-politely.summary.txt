This video discusses how Canada's banking regulator has formally warned major banks, particularly RBC, against using 'blanket appraisals'—a practice where properties are appraised based on purchase prices instead of current market values. This approach allows RBC to bypass loan-to-value regulations during a crashing condo market, effectively lending more than the law permits and mitigating risks for themselves but increasing systemic risk. Despite public knowledge and regulatory warnings, RBC's changes have been mostly superficial, highlighting the broader issue of regulatory leniency benefiting big banks and the real estate sector at the potential expense of other financial institutions and the market's integrity.
